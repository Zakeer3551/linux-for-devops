# Linux Fundamentals

Linux is the backbone of modern infrastructure, powering servers, cloud instances, CI/CD runners, and containerized environments. Understanding why Linux is widely preferred over other operating systems is critical for any DevOps or cloud engineer.

---

## Linux vs Windows: Key Advantages

### Cost-Effectiveness
- **Free and Open Source:** Linux requires no expensive licensing fees, making it highly cost-efficient for organizations of all sizes.  
- **Lower Maintenance Costs:** Its stability and minimal maintenance requirements reduce operational overhead.

### Performance and Efficiency
- **Better Resource Utilization:** Linux is lightweight and consumes fewer system resources than Windows.  
- **High Scalability:** It scales efficiently from small embedded systems to enterprise-grade data centers without significant performance degradation.

### Security and Reliability
- **Strong User Privilege Model:** Linux enforces strict separation of users and processes, making it less vulnerable to malware and unauthorized access.  
- **Frequent and Transparent Updates:** Security patches and updates are applied regularly, often without requiring system reboots.  
- **High Stability:** Linux systems can run for months or even years continuously, ensuring high uptime and reliability in production environments.
